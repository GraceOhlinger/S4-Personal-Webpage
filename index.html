My Work

Since I have no work experience in the graphic design field yet, I thought that I would take the opportunity to put in some of the work that I have designed while in school. This poster series, Home Is Where My Heart Is, was part of a final project for my Typography I class. Our final project was to create posters for a non-profit organization while putting meaning behind the typography we chose to use. I chose ASPCA because I care about animals and helping them get new homes. I am proud of how this project turned out. These posters helped me understand the meaning of typography more because of this project. This is one of my reasons why I am so interested in graphic design as a possible career because you learn something new every day.

For this poster series, I wanted to create the feeling that an animal may feel when it is in a loving home, a feeling that the animal wants to have for the rest of its life. So many animals today live without homes, which is an incredibly sad topic. I felt that if the posters revealed a more positive approach, the audience would want to help with the organization more. If the posters were to have a sad approach, there may be fewer people involved because of the feeling may they feel may have when viewing the poster.

Home Is Where My Heart Is (Heart) I felt that this poster expressed more of the loving side of animals. That they would do anything to be with a family that loves them. I chose a heart to replace the letter o in the word home because I felt that this design choice makes the poster more interesting and exciting then just the word home. Both the heart in the word home, and the word heart are the same shade of pink, which symbolizes the importance of a loving home. I learned that for this project, the information about the organization was secondary to the tagline or story that the poster is conveying to the viewer/audience.
Home Is Where My Heart Is (Pawprint) One thing that I appreciate with this poster is the variety it has, specifically with color. While I have a similar approach to this poster as I did with the previous one, I think that they both convey the same message in different ways. For example, I use a paw print instead of a heart. With this difference, I think it explains what the poster is about without looking for a logo whereas the heart doesn't explain everything right away. The word heart expresses a more gentle and caring approach versus the bold text with the word home symbolizing the main focus for both posters, which is a forever home for animals that need help.
